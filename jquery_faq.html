National Parks:

1) How much land does the National Park Service protect?
over 84 million acres of wild landscapes and historic sites.
2) Yellowstone National Park (in Wyoming, Idaho, and Montana) is the oldest U.S. national park, When was it founded?
Yellowstone National Park was founded in 1872.
3) Three of the 10 highest waterfalls in the world are located in which National Park?
Yosemite National Park in California
4) Ribbon Falls in Yosemite is nine times taller which water fall?
Niagara Falls.
5) One of the hottest temperatures on earth was recorded in 1913 in which national Park?
in Death Valley National Park, registering 134 degrees.
6) Which natural attraction at a US national park is known as one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World?
The Grand Canyon, in Grand Canyon National Park in Arizona
7) California's Sequoia National Park is home to the largest living single-stem tree in the world, What is it called?
named General Sherman. The tree is approximately 275 feet tall and weighs approximately 1,900 metric tons
8) When a giant sequoia tree fell and blocked a road in Sequoia National Park in California in 1937, how did the National park service deal with the problem?
the National Park Service simply created a tunnel through the 275-foot by 21-foot tree.
9) The second U.S. national park created is no longer a national park?
Mackinac National Park in Michigan was established in 1875 and was returned to the state in 1895.
10) The smallest national park is?
Hot Springs National Park in Arkansas, coming in at about 5,500 acres. This was actually the first federally protected piece of land in 1832. It was not named a national park until 1921.
